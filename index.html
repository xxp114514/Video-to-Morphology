A demonstration of the Image2Sim framework, which automates the conversion of raw video footage into simulatable Mujoco environments. The pipeline integrates DeepLabCut for kinematic extraction and LLM Vision for physics reasoning and XML assembly. The demo showcases the end-to-end workflow: from video input to agent generation, scene selection, and parallel Reinforcement Learning (RL) training experiments.

Abstract

Training an agent that originates from real-world videos has widely attracted attention. However, reproducing an agentâ€™s morphology and control policies in simulation remains a significant challenge. In this study, we propose a video-to-morphology learning pipeline (VM) that enables the reproduce agent's morphology and actions coming from real-world videos. The pipeline consists of two main components: The initialization stage responsible for reconstructing agent's morphology and the background environment. The control optimization stage consists of a three-tier loop strategy which are environment curriculum learning, morphological adjustment and reward adaptation. We reproduce several simulations coming from real-world videos. Experimental results demonstrate that removing any module from the control optimization stage results in at least a 10\% decrease in task success rate. Overall, this work provides a robust pathway for translating real-world Video into simulations.
